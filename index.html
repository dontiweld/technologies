    About Us

    Donti Weld Technologies specialises in the bulk manufacture of consumables used in welding applications throughout various industries, primarily manufacturing standard E6013 welding electrodes. The manufacturing facility is located in Bengaluru South.

    Our History

    Donti Weld Technology was founded in the year 2004, as a proprietorship by Mr. Sanath Kumar. Through the experience gained from working as the cofounder and Managing Director at Donthi Electrodes Private Limited, Mysore, he established the new company in Bengaluru South, on self owned land. Mr. Sanath Kumar graduated in the year 1982, with a degree in Mechanical Engineering. He worked as a trade dealer of commercial brands of electrodes from 1985 before starting his first company.

    Our Position

    At present, Donti Weld Technologies manufactures welding electrodes coated in specially formulated flux that provides a clean weld with minimal slag. The electrodes are manufactured in 3 different sizes and quantities, meant for use in several different industries. As of now, the current factory has a manufacturing capacity of 3 metric tons per shift, with 6 shifts per week, and employs many workers as a part of the manufacturing line, quality assurance, and logistics.

    Our Vision

    Here at Donti Weld Technologies, our vision has always been to provide the highest quality products for use in industries at a competitive price.     Safety Information

    We perform quality control of our products by inspecting the quality of our specially formulated flux coating, which yields a clean weld. When welding, it is important to adhere to the following safety precautions:

    Electrode Bending

    Do not bend electrodes, as it may crack the coating.

    Welding Angle

    Angle of electrode should be 60° - 80° to the work surface. Use a short arc, as a longer arc will result in more spatter, excessive smoke and defective weld.

    Earthing

    Earth properly for safety. Without proper earthing, striking of arc will not be easy.

    Welding Position

    Weld in down hand position wherever possible, do not weld at an awkward angle.

    Starting

    Faulty start with electrode often results in defects, make sure starting point is smooth. Go back 0.5 inches and then move forward. Do not start weld at corner of weld joint.

    Cleaning

    Clean weld joint with wire brush or grinder to be free of dirt, rust, oil and grease.

    Slag Removal

    Use chipping hammer with freshly ground fine edge at one end and sharp point at the other, slag removal will be easier.

    Welding Current

    Select proper welding current depending on thickness of plate, the position of welding, size of electrode and welder comfort. Do not over weld, as this will result in distortion of the joint.

    Safety Equipment

    Wear protective clothing, gloves, shoes and use a helmet with clean glass along with good quality holder and welding cable, In open areas, use large shield to protect from wind.
